title: Build your first app

---

You will create a working app on OnMicro.AI by describing what you want in plain language. The **App Builder** drafts the app for you; you review the result, make a few tweaks, and try it in **Preview**.

## Prerequisites

- An [OnMicro.AI](https://onmicro.ai) account. Sign up or log in at [onmicro.ai/accounts/login](https://onmicro.ai/accounts/login).

## Step 1 — Create a new app

1. Open your **dashboard** at [onmicro.ai/dashboard](https://onmicro.ai/dashboard).
2. Click **Create App** in the top navigation, or **Create your first app** if your dashboard is empty.

You land on the **Build** tab for a new, private app titled **Untitled App**. The left panel shows **App settings**; the center shows **App Details** and a welcome card.

## Step 2 — Describe what you want to build

In the welcome card, **Describe what you want to build**:

1. Type a short description in the text box. Be specific about the learner experience — for example:

   > Build me a multiple-choice quiz generator. The learner enters a topic and grade level, then gets five questions with four answer choices each.

2. Or click one of the **starter ideas** to fill the box (for example, **Build me a Multiple Choice Question Generator**), then edit the text if you like.

3. Click **Continue with App Builder**.

The welcome card closes and the **App Builder** panel opens on the right. Your description is sent automatically.

**Checkpoint:** You should see **App Builder** at the top of the right panel, with the subtitle *AI-powered app generation*, and your message in the chat.

## Step 3 — Wait while App Builder drafts your app

App Builder works in the background:

1. It may show **Thinking** (click to expand) while it plans.
2. It posts a short plan in plain language, then updates your app.
3. When finished, you see **App updated successfully** under the reply.

**Checkpoint:** New sections and fields appear in the center of the **Build** tab — for example, text inputs, dropdowns, or an **AI Response** block. Your app is saved automatically; you may briefly see **Saving...** in the header.

If App Builder cannot help with your request (for example, a question unrelated to building apps), it replies with a brief notice instead of changing your app. Rephrase your request to describe the app you want.

## Step 4 — Refine with chat

Use the App Builder chat to iterate:

1. Type what to change in the box at the bottom — for example:

   > Add a boolean toggle for "Include answer explanations."

2. Press **Enter** to send ( **Shift+Enter** for a new line).

3. Wait for **App updated successfully** again.

If a change is not what you wanted, click **Undo** in the App Builder header to restore the app to before the last AI update. You can undo up to the last several changes in your session.

To reopen App Builder after closing it, click the **App Builder** button on the right side of the **Build** tab.

## Step 5 — Name your app

1. In the **App Details** card at the top of the **Build** tab, click the title area.
2. Replace **Untitled App** with a clear name learners will recognize.
3. Add a short description under the title — this text appears on the run screen when someone opens your app.

Changes save automatically.

## Step 6 — Preview your app

1. Click **Preview** in the center navigation bar.
2. Walk through the app as a learner would: fill in fields and click **Continue** (or **Evaluate** on scored steps) to move through each section.
3. Confirm the flow matches what you described.

If something is wrong, switch back to **Build**, adjust with App Builder or by editing individual fields, then preview again.

## What you have now

You created an app from a description, refined it in chat, named it, and tested it in Preview. Your app is private by default.

**Next:** [Share your app](/tutorials/share-your-app) to publish it with a link or embed code, or open the **Share** tab to change privacy settings.
