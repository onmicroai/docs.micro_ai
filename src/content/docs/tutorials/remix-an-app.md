---
title: Remix an app
description: Copy a public app you like into your own OnMicro.AI account and customize it — using the Remix banner or Clone action.
---

You will take someone else's app, create your own copy, and open it in the editor to customize. Remixing duplicates the full app design — components, prompts, and settings — into a new app you own.

## Prerequisites

- An [OnMicro.AI](https://onmicro.ai) account. You can sign up during the remix flow if you do not have one yet.
- A **public** app that allows remixing. The app owner must have **Allow others to clone this app** enabled (this is on by default for most apps).

## Step 1 — Find an app to remix

Browse the **Public MicroApps Library** at [onmicro.ai/library](https://onmicro.ai/library).

1. Scroll the list of featured public apps.
2. Read the descriptions and pick one that fits what you want to build on.
3. Click **Launch App** on a card to open it.

You can also remix from any **public** app link someone shared with you, as long as cloning is allowed.

**Checkpoint:** The app opens on its run screen with the app title and description at the top.

## Step 2 — Try the app

Walk through the app as a learner would. Confirm it is close to what you want before you remix — your copy starts from the same design.

You do not need to finish the entire flow, but spend enough time to know the app is a good starting point.

## Step 3 — Click Remix this app

A banner appears at the bottom of the screen:

> **Like this app?** Create your own version by remixing it.

1. Click **Remix this app →**.

The banner only appears when:

- The app owner allows cloning, and
- You are not the owner or admin of that app (owners editing their own app do not see it).

You can dismiss the banner with **X** if you are not ready to remix yet. It stays hidden for the rest of that browser session.

If cloning is disabled, the banner does not appear and remix is not available for that app.

## Step 4 — Sign in (if prompted)

If you are not logged in, OnMicro.AI sends you to the login page. Your remix is remembered.

1. Sign in or create an account.
2. After login, OnMicro.AI automatically creates your copy — you do not need to click **Remix this app** again.

If you were already logged in, your copy is created immediately when you click **Remix this app**.

## Step 5 — Confirm your copy

After remix completes, you land on the run screen for **your** new app.

**Checkpoint:** Look for these signs you have your own copy:

- The title ends with **copy** (for example, *MCQ Generator copy*).
- **Edit this app** appears in the top-right corner of the run screen.
- The remix banner is gone — you are now the owner.

Your copy also appears on your [dashboard](https://onmicro.ai/dashboard).

If remix fails (for example, you reached your account's app limit), an error message appears. Resolve the issue and try again from the original app.

## Step 6 — Open the editor

1. Click **Edit this app** on the run screen.

You arrive on the **Build** tab for your copy. Everything from the original — components, AI prompts, and app settings — is yours to change.

Alternatively, find the app on your dashboard and click **Edit**.

## Step 7 — Make it yours

Customize the copy so it fits your learners and context:

1. Update **App Details** — rename the app (remove **copy** from the title) and write your own description.
2. Adjust components or use **App Builder** to describe changes in plain language. See [Edit an app](/tutorials/edit-app).
3. Review **App settings** — set **Privacy Settings** to **Private** while you work, then **Public** when you are ready to share. See [Configure app settings](/tutorials/configure-app-settings).
4. Click **Preview** to test your version before sharing.

## What you have now

You started from a public app, remixed it into your account, and opened your own editable copy. The original app is unchanged — you have an independent version to adapt and share.

**Next:** Share your version from the [Share your app](/tutorials/share-your-app) tutorial, or open the **Share** tab in the editor.

## Allow others to remix your apps

If you publish apps for others to learn from, keep **Allow others to clone this app** checked in **App settings** so visitors see the remix banner. Turn it off for apps you do not want copied. See [Configure app settings](/tutorials/configure-app-settings).
